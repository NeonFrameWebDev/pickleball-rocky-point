No app, no account, no booking. The hardest part is leaving. Here is the whole thing.

03
The courts

Four courts. One sunset.

Blue acrylic courts with crisp white lines and regulation nets, shaded by big sails and lit for night play. Plus a volleyball court when you want to switch it up.

four courts, ready to rally

Built to play.

  • Four dedicated pickleball courts, blue acrylic with regulation nets.
  • Big shade sails overhead so you are out of the midday sun.
  • Floodlights on every court for play after dark.
  • Open play, all welcome, locals and visitors alike.
the octopus wall

Only-in-Rocky-Point character.

A hand-painted octopus mural runs the back wall, palms line the fences, and the Sea of Cortez light does the rest. Play your best 6 PM game of the trip, then keep going under the lights.

and a volleyball court

Bring a crew.

There is a volleyball court too, so a big group can rotate between pickleball, volleyball, and the shade. One spot, all afternoon, one flat price.

The hangout

Way more than courts.

Between games there is shade, seats, cold-drink weather, and stuff to do. It is a hangout, not just a court.

rest, regroup, rematch

A real clubhouse.

A covered pavilion and a shaded indoor lounge with Mexican talavera tile, picnic tables, a foosball table, and a TV. Sit out a game, cool off, and watch the next rally.

06
Good to know

Questions, answered.

Do I need to book or reserve?

No. It is first come, open play. Just show up and pay 100 pesos per player.

How much does it cost?

100 pesos per player for the whole day. No hourly rates and no tiers.

Do I need to bring my own paddle?

Yes. Bring your own paddle and ball. There are no rentals on site, so come ready to play.

How many courts are there?

Four dedicated pickleball courts, plus a volleyball court.

Can I play at night?

Yes. Every court is lit, so you can play after dark. Stay for the sunset and keep going.

Is it open to the public and visitors?

Yes, everyone is welcome. Locals and visitors, all skill levels.

Where exactly are you?

Calle Luis Encinas Johnson 439, Ferrocarrilera, Puerto Penasco. See the map above for directions.

Lessons, clinics, or leagues?

Open play is the main thing right now. Message us if you are interested in lessons, leagues, or group play.
